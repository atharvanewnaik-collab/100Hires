The discussion focuses on **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)**, AI visibility, Google's AI Overviews, and the evolving role of SEO as users increasingly receive synthesized answers instead of traditional search results.

Although the conversation includes ecommerce examples, the strategic principles apply broadly to organizations seeking sustainable visibility in AI-powered search ecosystems, including B2B SaaS companies.

## Summary

Aleyda Solís explains how generative AI is changing the way users discover information online. With AI-powered search experiences such as Google's AI Overviews providing direct answers, SEO professionals must adapt their strategies beyond traditional ranking optimization and consider how content is retrieved, interpreted, and cited by AI systems.

A central theme of the discussion is **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)**—optimizing content so that it is more likely to be referenced within AI-generated responses. Rather than replacing traditional SEO, GEO builds upon strong technical foundations, high-quality content, and clear topical authority.

The conversation also examines how organizations should measure visibility in an environment where users may receive answers without clicking through to websites. Aleyda emphasizes the importance of monitoring AI search experiences, understanding citation patterns, and creating authoritative, trustworthy content that satisfies user intent.

While many examples focus on ecommerce, the underlying recommendations—including producing expert content, maintaining technical excellence, and building topical authority—are equally applicable to B2B SaaS organizations adapting to AI-powered search. ([youtube.com](http://youtube.com))

## Key Insights

- AI-powered search is changing how users discover and consume information.
- **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** complements traditional SEO by improving visibility within AI-generated responses.
- Technical SEO remains an essential foundation for AI visibility, ensuring content can be effectively crawled, indexed, and understood.
- High-quality, authoritative, and trustworthy content is more likely to be surfaced and cited by AI-powered search systems.
- Topical authority and comprehensive coverage of user needs are increasingly important as AI synthesizes information from multiple sources.
- SEO teams should expand success metrics beyond clicks and rankings to include visibility within AI search experiences and generated answers.
- Organizations should continuously monitor AI-powered search interfaces to understand how their content and brand are represented.
- AI should enhance—not replace—strategic SEO practices built around user intent, technical excellence, and valuable content. ([youtube.com](http://youtube.com))
