# How to Win AI Search Shift

## Overview

In this webinar, Ethan Smith (CEO of Graphite) and Alex Halliday (CEO of AirOps) discuss how AI-powered search is changing the way users discover information and how businesses should adapt their SEO and content strategies. The conversation explores the transition from traditional search optimization to AI Search, emphasizing visibility within large language models (LLMs), AI-generated answers, and emerging search interfaces.

The session combines strategic insights with practical guidance, helping marketing teams understand how to build content that performs across both traditional search engines and AI-powered discovery platforms.

## Summary

Ethan Smith explains that search is undergoing a structural shift as users increasingly rely on AI-powered experiences that generate direct answers instead of returning lists of webpages. This evolution requires SEO teams to rethink how success is measured and how content is produced.

The discussion introduces practical approaches for improving visibility within AI-generated responses. Rather than optimizing solely for keyword rankings, organizations should focus on creating authoritative, well-structured, and semantically complete content that AI systems can easily retrieve, understand, and reference. The speakers also examine how traditional SEO principles—including technical quality, topical authority, and user-focused content—remain important while being adapted for generative search.

A recurring theme is that AI should enhance editorial workflows rather than replace human expertise. By combining AI-assisted research and content production with rigorous editorial review, businesses can scale high-quality content while maintaining trust and accuracy.

For B2B SaaS organizations, the webinar highlights the importance of building durable content systems that perform across both conventional search engines and emerging AI-powered discovery experiences.

## Key Insights

- AI-powered search is changing how users discover information and reducing reliance on traditional search results.
- Visibility within LLM-generated answers is becoming an important complement to conventional search rankings.
- High-quality, authoritative, and semantically complete content is more likely to be retrieved and cited by AI systems.
- Topical authority remains a competitive advantage in both traditional and AI-powered search.
- AI-assisted content production should be integrated into structured editorial workflows with human oversight.
- Measuring search success increasingly requires monitoring AI visibility in addition to rankings and organic traffic.
- Technical SEO, content quality, and clear information architecture continue to support discoverability in AI search environments.
- Organizations should build repeatable content systems that can adapt as AI-powered search continues to evolve.
