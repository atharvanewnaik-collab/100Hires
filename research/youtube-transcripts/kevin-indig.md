# Google Will Kill Your Traffic — Here's How You Adapt

## Overview

In this episode of the Ahrefs Podcast, Kevin Indig discusses how AI-powered search is reshaping SEO and why organic traffic can no longer be the primary measure of success. The conversation explores the impact of Google's AI Overviews, the rise of LLM-powered search experiences, changing user behavior, and how B2B SaaS companies should evolve their content strategies to remain visible in an AI-first search landscape.

Rather than treating AI as a threat to SEO, Kevin argues that it fundamentally changes how marketers should measure success, create content, and build brand authority.

## Summary

Kevin Indig explains that AI-powered search experiences, particularly Google's AI Overviews, are reducing organic clicks by answering more user questions directly within search results. As a result, many organizations should expect declining website traffic even when maintaining strong search visibility.

Instead of optimizing exclusively for rankings and clicks, SEO teams should focus on broader business outcomes such as qualified leads, conversions, brand awareness, and visibility across AI-powered search platforms. Kevin emphasizes that search is evolving from a list of blue links into an ecosystem where users discover information through AI-generated answers, community platforms, videos, and trusted brands.

The discussion also highlights the growing importance of authority, trust, and content quality. AI systems increasingly rely on reputable sources when generating answers, making brand credibility a competitive advantage. Kevin recommends creating genuinely useful content informed by customer problems, maintaining content freshness, and expanding visibility beyond a company's own website.

For B2B SaaS organizations, this represents a shift from maximizing traffic volume to maximizing business impact through high-intent content, strong brand recognition, and diversified search visibility.

## Key Insights

- AI Overviews are changing search behavior by providing answers directly within search results, reducing clicks to traditional webpages.
- Organic traffic should no longer be treated as the primary KPI for SEO success.
- Business metrics such as revenue, qualified leads, pipeline contribution, and conversions are becoming more meaningful success indicators.
- AI-generated search results place greater emphasis on authority, trustworthiness, and content quality.
- Brand recognition becomes increasingly valuable because users are more likely to trust familiar brands when validating AI-generated answers.
- High-intent, long-tail queries continue to offer strong opportunities despite declining overall search traffic.
- SEO is expanding beyond Google to include AI assistants and other discovery platforms, requiring a broader visibility strategy.
- Customer conversations and first-party insights can produce more valuable content ideas than relying solely on keyword research.
- AI should be used to accelerate research, analysis, and workflow efficiency while maintaining human editorial oversight for accuracy and quality.
- Organizations should prioritize creating unique, experience-based content that AI systems are more likely to reference and users are more likely to trust.
