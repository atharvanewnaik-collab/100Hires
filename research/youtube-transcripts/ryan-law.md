# How to Automate Blog Writing with AI from Keyword to Published

## Overview

In this Ahrefs Podcast episode, Ryan Law demonstrates the end-to-end AI-assisted content production workflow used by the Ahrefs content team. Rather than presenting AI as a replacement for writers, he shows how a structured editorial system combines large language models with documented processes and human review to produce high-quality, search-focused content at scale.

The video provides a practical blueprint for integrating AI into B2B SaaS content operations, covering every stage from keyword research and content briefing to drafting, editing, internal linking, formatting, and publication.

## Summary

Ryan Law walks through the complete AI-powered editorial workflow developed by the Ahrefs content team. The process is built around a series of documented editorial instructions that guide an LLM through each stage of content creation, including research, briefing, outlining, drafting, editing, formatting, internal linking, and publication.

Rather than relying on a single prompt, the workflow breaks content production into specialized steps, each supported by dedicated process documents and quality guidelines. This modular approach enables AI to assist with repetitive and time-consuming tasks while preserving editorial consistency and factual accuracy.

A recurring theme throughout the discussion is that human expertise remains essential. AI accelerates research, drafting, and formatting, but experienced editors are responsible for validating claims, improving clarity, refining structure, and ensuring the content reflects the company's standards and expertise.

Ryan also emphasizes that effective AI content production depends more on building robust systems than on finding the "perfect prompt." By documenting editorial knowledge and converting it into repeatable workflows, organizations can scale content production without sacrificing quality.

---

## Key Insights

- High-quality AI content is produced through structured workflows rather than one-shot prompting.
- Editorial knowledge should be documented as repeatable process documents that AI can consistently follow.
- The workflow divides content production into separate stages such as briefing, outlining, drafting, editing, internal linking, formatting, and publishing.
- Human editors remain responsible for fact-checking, quality assurance, strategic judgment, and maintaining editorial standards.
- AI performs best when each task has a clearly defined objective and supporting instructions.
- Modular workflows improve consistency, reduce hallucinations, and make content easier to review and update.
- Internal linking, metadata, formatting, and product integration can all be incorporated into an AI-assisted publishing pipeline.
- The goal is not fully autonomous content generation but a scalable human-in-the-loop editorial system that increases productivity while maintaining quality.
