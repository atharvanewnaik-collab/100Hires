# Leveling Up SEO for the AI Era

## Overview

In this presentation, Tom Critchlow examines how artificial intelligence is reshaping the practice of SEO and the role of SEO professionals. Rather than viewing AI as simply another productivity tool, he argues that it fundamentally changes how marketers research, create, analyze, and make strategic decisions.

The talk focuses on adapting SEO teams and workflows for an AI-first environment, emphasizing that long-term competitive advantage will come from combining AI capabilities with human judgment, domain expertise, and strategic thinking.

## Summary

Tom Critchlow explores how AI is transforming knowledge work and what this means for SEO professionals. He argues that AI should not be viewed as a replacement for SEO expertise but as a powerful collaborator that enables marketers to spend less time on repetitive tasks and more time on strategic thinking, experimentation, and solving complex business problems.

A key theme throughout the presentation is that the value of SEO is shifting away from executing isolated optimization tasks toward building systems, frameworks, and processes that integrate AI effectively. Rather than relying on AI to generate content autonomously, organizations should develop workflows where AI accelerates research, ideation, analysis, and documentation while humans provide context, judgment, and business alignment.

Tom also discusses the changing nature of search as AI-powered interfaces become more prominent. Success increasingly depends on understanding user intent, creating genuinely useful information, and adapting organizational workflows rather than chasing algorithmic shortcuts.

For B2B SaaS organizations, the presentation highlights the importance of developing AI-enabled knowledge systems that improve decision-making, increase productivity, and support sustainable organic growth.

---

## Key Insights

- AI changes how SEO work is performed rather than eliminating the need for SEO professionals.
- The highest-value SEO work increasingly involves strategic thinking, experimentation, and organizational problem-solving.
- AI is most effective when integrated into repeatable workflows instead of being used for isolated prompting.
- Human expertise remains essential for interpreting AI outputs, making business decisions, and maintaining quality.
- Organizations should build knowledge systems that combine AI capabilities with documented processes and institutional expertise.
- SEO teams should invest in learning how AI changes research, planning, and decision-making rather than focusing solely on content generation.
- Competitive advantage will come from developing unique workflows and organizational capabilities rather than using the same AI tools as everyone else.
- AI should augment human creativity and strategic judgment while automating repetitive operational tasks.
